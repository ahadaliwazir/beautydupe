## The Original: Charlotte Tilbury Hollywood Flawless Filter

Charlotte Tilbury's Hollywood Flawless Filter ($49) has achieved cult status for good reason. It's a hybrid primer-highlighter that gives skin a filtered, luminous look without glitter or shimmer. You can wear it alone, under foundation, or mixed in. The effect is a soft-focus, lit-from-within glow that looks like great skin rather than obvious product.

But $49 for 30ml of a product you use daily adds up fast. We tested the most popular dupes head-to-head.

## Dupe #1: e.l.f. Halo Glow Liquid Filter — $14

**The closest match.** The e.l.f. Halo Glow has basically the same concept: a light-coverage, luminous complexion booster with a soft-focus finish. The texture is slightly thinner than CT (more liquid, less serum-like), but once blended, the effect on skin is remarkably similar.

- **Finish:** Dewy, luminous, natural
- **Coverage:** Light — evens tone without masking skin
- **Shade range:** 8 shades (better than CT's original launch range)
- **Longevity:** About 6 hours before needing a touch-up (CT lasts ~8)
- **Match to original:** 85%

## Dupe #2: L'Oréal Lumi Glotion — $12

**The budget champion.** This one has been around longer than the CT hype and does a similar job. It's more of a tinted moisturizer-meets-illuminator. The texture is lighter and more moisturizing, making it ideal for dry skin types.

- **Finish:** Natural glow, slightly more dewy than CT
- **Coverage:** Sheer to light
- **Shade range:** 5 shades
- **Longevity:** About 5-6 hours
- **Match to original:** 70%

The Glotion leans more "moisturizer" while CT leans more "primer," but the visual effect is similar.

## Dupe #3: NYX Born to Glow Radiant Foundation — $10 (on sale)

**Different category, similar result.** This is technically a radiant foundation, but when applied with a light hand (one pump, blended with a damp sponge), it gives a similar filtered, healthy-skin look. You get slightly more coverage than the CT, which can be a pro or con depending on your preference.

- **Finish:** Radiant, skin-like
- **Coverage:** Light to medium (use less for a CT dupe effect)
- **Shade range:** 45 shades (the winner by far)
- **Longevity:** About 7 hours
- **Match to original:** 65% (different product type, similar vibe)

## The Verdict

**Buy the e.l.f. Halo Glow** if you want the closest possible dupe experience. It's $14 and delivers 85% of the CT effect. The only real sacrifice is longevity.

**Buy the Charlotte Tilbury** if you wear it daily and care about the last 2 hours of wear time, or if you love the original's unique gel-serum texture.

**Buy the L'Oréal Glotion** if you have dry skin and want the most hydrating option.

All three dupes are genuinely good products in their own right — the CT tax is mostly packaging, brand prestige, and slightly better longevity.
