## Our Testing Approach

We tested five electric razors over six months — spending at least 30 days with each as our sole shaving tool. We evaluated closeness of shave, skin comfort, battery life, ease of cleaning, and long-term durability. No razor on this list was provided free by a brand.

## #1: Braun Series 9 Pro+ — $399

**Best overall for closeness and comfort.**

The Series 9 Pro+ consistently delivered the closest electric shave we've experienced. Its five shaving elements (including a titanium-coated trimmer) catch everything in 1-2 passes, even against the grain on the neck.

- **Closeness:** 9/10 (for an electric; still not blade-level)
- **Comfort:** 9/10 — almost zero irritation even on sensitive neck areas
- **Battery:** 60 minutes; fast charge gives 1 shave in 5 minutes
- **Cleaning:** Auto-clean station works well; refills cost ~$6/month
- **Best for:** Daily shavers who want the closest possible electric shave

**The downside:** $399 is a lot, and replacement cassettes ($30 every 18 months) add up. But if you shave daily and value your mornings, it pays for itself in 2 years vs. quality blade cartridges.

## #2: Philips Norelco 9500 — $230

**Best rotary razor; ideal for longer growth.**

If you shave every 2-3 days rather than daily, the Norelco's rotary heads handle longer stubble more gracefully than foil razors. The flexible heads contour to facial curves naturally.

- **Closeness:** 7.5/10 (rotary trade-off: less close, more comfortable)
- **Comfort:** 9.5/10 — the gentlest option tested
- **Battery:** 60 minutes; 1-hour full charge
- **Cleaning:** Included cleaning pod; basic but functional
- **Best for:** Guys with sensitive skin who shave 2-3x per week

## #3: Braun Series 7 — $150

**Best value for quality.**

About 85% of the Series 9's performance for 40% of the price. The Series 7 has four shaving elements instead of five, so you might need an extra pass on tough spots, but the overall experience is still premium.

- **Closeness:** 8/10
- **Comfort:** 8.5/10
- **Battery:** 50 minutes
- **Cleaning:** Compatible with auto-clean station (sold separately)
- **Best for:** First-time premium electric razor buyers; the sweet spot of price and performance

## #4: Panasonic Arc5 ES-LV67 — $130

**Best for wet shaving.**

If you like to shave in the shower or with shaving cream, the Panasonic Arc5 is designed for it. The five-blade foil system is aggressive (very close) but can be irritating for sensitive skin when used dry.

- **Closeness:** 9/10 (closest in our test; rivals a blade)
- **Comfort:** 7/10 dry, 8.5/10 wet (needs cream/gel for comfort)
- **Battery:** 45 minutes
- **Cleaning:** Manual only (no station); simple pop-open head
- **Best for:** Wet shavers who want electric convenience with blade-like closeness

## #5: Braun Series 5 — $79

**Best budget option worth buying.**

Below $80, electric razors get dicey. The Series 5 is the floor for quality — it delivers a good (not great) shave with decent comfort. You'll need more passes than pricier options, but it gets the job done.

- **Closeness:** 7/10
- **Comfort:** 7.5/10
- **Battery:** 50 minutes
- **Cleaning:** Manual; EasyClean system makes it simple
- **Best for:** Budget-conscious buyers or those new to electric shaving

## Our Recommendation

**Most people should buy the Braun Series 7** ($150). It's the point of diminishing returns — spending more gets you incremental improvements, but the Series 7 delivers a genuinely premium shaving experience at a reasonable price.

**If budget is no object:** The Series 9 Pro+ is noticeably better, and if you shave daily for years, the cost-per-shave premium is trivial.

**If you're just trying electric:** Start with the Series 5 at $79. If you like electric shaving, upgrade to the Series 7 in a year or two.
