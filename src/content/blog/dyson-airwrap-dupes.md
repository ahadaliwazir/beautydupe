## Can You Really Dupe the Airwrap?

The Dyson Airwrap ($599) uses a patented high-velocity motor and the Coanda effect to style hair with air rather than extreme heat. At its core, it's a powerful blow dryer with clever barrel attachments that attract hair and wrap it automatically.

The dupes we tested take a different approach: they use lower-powered motors combined with more traditional heat to create curls with similar-looking barrels. The results look similar, but the mechanism (and heat damage potential) differs.

## How We Tested

Each dupe was tested on the same model over two weeks, alternating between devices. We evaluated: curl quality, lasting power, heat damage (assessed by feel and elasticity test), ease of use, and noise level.

## Best Overall: Shark FlexStyle ($60 refurbished / on sale)

Technically above our budget at full retail ($270), the Shark FlexStyle frequently goes on sale or is available refurbished for around $60. If you can grab it at that price, it's the definitive Airwrap alternative.

- **Curl quality:** Excellent — closest to Dyson we've tested
- **Heat control:** Intelligent heat regulation similar to Dyson
- **Attachments:** Curling barrel, oval brush, paddle brush, concentrator
- **Longevity of curls:** 8-10 hours
- **Our take:** If you find it under $80, buy it immediately

## Best Under $50: Lanpro Auto-Wrap Styler — $45

This is the best pure dupe experience under $50. The barrel design mimics the Airwrap's shape and the automatic wrap feature (assisted by small vents) works surprisingly well once you get the technique down.

- **Curl quality:** Good — slightly less uniform than Dyson
- **Heat levels:** 3 settings; highest is hotter than we'd like
- **Attachments:** 2 curling barrels (different sizes), smoothing brush
- **Longevity of curls:** 6-8 hours
- **Our take:** Best balance of price and performance for most people

## Budget Pick: Wavytalk 5-in-1 Styler — $36

At $36, expectations should be calibrated. But this device surprised us with decent curl formation and a full set of attachments. The motor is weaker, so styling takes longer, and the curls are less tight.

- **Curl quality:** Decent — more loose waves than defined curls
- **Heat levels:** 2 settings; runs warm but not concerning
- **Attachments:** 5 pieces including curling barrel, brushes, and concentrator
- **Longevity of curls:** 4-6 hours (use hairspray)
- **Our take:** Fine for occasional use; don't expect daily-driver reliability

## Skip: Generic "Airwrap Dupes" on Amazon Under $25

We tested two no-name $20 devices with thousands of reviews. Both were disappointing: weak motors that couldn't wrap hair at all, extreme heat on the highest setting, and one that smelled like burning plastic after 5 minutes. Don't waste your money.

## Bottom Line

No sub-$60 tool truly replicates the Dyson Airwrap experience — the engineering gap is real. But if you want Airwrap-style results on a budget:

- **Grab the Shark FlexStyle on sale** if you can find it under $80
- **Buy the Lanpro** for the best everyday experience under $50
- **Get the Wavytalk** if you only curl your hair occasionally

If you style daily and can afford it, the Dyson remains worth it for the reduced heat damage alone. But for 80% of people, a $45 dupe delivers 80% of the experience — and that math works out fine.
